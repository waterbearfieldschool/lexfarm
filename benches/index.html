Goatyard Benches

We're working with Goodwin Hill Goats to help LexFarm build capacity for a larger goat herd in the fall.

One of our first projects is to design and build some benches that both goats and humans can use. We'd like the design to be sturdy, inexpensive, built from readily available or scrap lumber using simple tools, and easily assembled by kids and adults with little woodworking experience.

Candidate Design #1

We've now come up with what we think is a nice starter for a goat bench!

The top of the bench is 42″ long, 22″ wide, and 17″ high. In total this design requires six 2×4s, at an approximate cost of $30.

The bench 'base'

The base of the bench is made from three 2×4s, at a cost of about $15. The pieces can be cut, and perhaps pre-assembled, in advance of a workshop.

Options for the seat

Once the sturdy base is assembled, the seat of the bench can be varied to accommodate design preferences or available materials.
